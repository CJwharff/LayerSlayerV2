Solver Logic Walkthrough

Equation sequence implemented by the multilayer stress, curvature, and delamination solver.

1. Steady-State Temperature Distribution

For the thermal workflow, each layer is treated as one-dimensional steady conduction. Since \( \partial^2 T / \partial y^2 = 0 \) within each layer, the temperature is linear through each layer and jumps across interfaces with finite conductance.

\[ R_{\mathrm{total}} = \sum_{i=1}^{N}\frac{h_i}{k_i} + \sum_{j=1}^{N-1}\frac{1}{k_{\mathrm{int},j}}, \qquad q = \frac{T_{\mathrm{top}} - T_{\mathrm{bottom}}}{R_{\mathrm{total}}} \]

The heat flux \(q\) is uniform. The temperature rise through a layer is \(q h_i/k_i\), and the interface jump is \(q/k_{\mathrm{int},j}\). Walking from bottom to top gives the temperature at every layer boundary.

2. Mismatch Strain and Mechanical Kinematics

Mismatch strain is the free strain accumulated from the reference, stress-free state to the operating state. In thermal mode, the solver computes it from the local temperature field. In direct mismatch mode, it is supplied directly.

\[ \theta_i(y) = \alpha_i\left[T_i(y)-T_{\mathrm{ref},i}\right] \] \[ \varepsilon(y) = \varepsilon_0 - \kappa y \]

The unknowns are the bottom strain \( \varepsilon_0 \) and curvature \( \kappa \). Plane sections remain plane, so the strain field is linear through the stack.

3. Constitutive Response

The stress in each layer subtracts the mismatch strain from the mechanical strain. The effective modulus and mismatch coefficient depend on the assumed constraint condition.

\[ \sigma_i(y)=\bar{E}_i\left[\varepsilon_0-\kappa y-\bar{c}_i\theta_i(y)\right] \] \[ \text{Biaxial:}\quad \bar{E}_i=\frac{E_i}{1-\nu_i},\quad \bar{c}_i=1 \] \[ \text{Plane strain:}\quad \bar{E}_i=\frac{E_i}{1-\nu_i^2},\quad \bar{c}_i=1+\nu_i \]

4. Force and Moment Equilibrium

The intact multilayer stack is free of resultant force and moment unless external resultants \(N_0\) and \(M_0\) are prescribed. Integrating the stress field through each layer gives a \(2 \times 2\) linear system for \( \varepsilon_0 \) and \( \kappa \).

\[ a_{11}\varepsilon_0 + a_{12}\kappa = b_1 + N_0 \] \[ a_{21}\varepsilon_0 + a_{22}\kappa = b_2 + M_0 \] \[ a_{11}=\sum_i \bar{E}_i h_i, \qquad a_{22}=\sum_i \bar{E}_i\frac{y_{t,i}^3-y_{b,i}^3}{3} \]

The terms \(b_1\) and \(b_2\) are the mismatch-strain load resultants. Solving this system gives the curvature and stress state used in the output plots.

5. Delamination Driving Force

For each interface, the solver uses the change-problem/superposition approach from Begley and Hutchinson. The intact stress field is integrated over the lower and upper sub-stacks to obtain force and moment resultants. When a crack forms, the change problem releases those resultants with zero mismatch strain.

\[ G = \frac{j}{2}\sum_{\mathrm{substacks}} \left(\Delta N\,\Delta\varepsilon_0 + \Delta M\,\Delta\kappa\right) \]

The factor \(j\) reflects the cracking constraint: \(j=1\) for plane strain and \(j=2\) for equi-biaxial. The app reports three modes: Classic, Biaxial, and Plane Strain.

Transient Solver Walkthrough

Transient multilayer transport process derived from Begley & Hutchinson Chapters 13 and 15.

1. Governing Transport Equation

Each layer is treated as one-dimensional transient diffusion through the thickness. The dependent variable may be concentration for species transport or temperature for heat conduction.

\[ \beta_i\frac{\partial c_i}{\partial t} = D_i\frac{\partial^2 c_i}{\partial y^2} - k_i\,c_i + S_i \]

\(\beta_i\) is the storage coefficient (reversible pore storage; default 1). \(D_i\) is the diffusivity. \(k_i \geq 0\) is a first-order volumetric sink (consumption). \(S_i\) is a constant volumetric source (production).

2. Layer Discretization

Following the Chapter 15 transient framework, each physical layer is subdivided into uniform through-thickness elements. Within each element, the concentration or temperature profile is approximated as linear.

\[ c(y,t) \approx N_1(y)c_1(t)+N_2(y)c_2(t) \]

Adjacent layers share the same interface node in this first implementation, which enforces concentration continuity directly.

3. Flux Continuity and Assembly

Element mass and diffusion matrices are assembled into one global system. This conserves flux across internal nodes, so the interface flux leaving one layer balances the flux entering the next.

\[ [C_\beta]\{\dot{c}\} + \left([K_D]+[M_k]\right)\{c\} = \{s\} + \{q(t)\} \]

This mirrors the Chapter 15 transient form \( [C]\{\dot{T}\} + [K]\{T\} = \{q(t)\} \), generalized here to concentration or temperature and augmented with the course-note storage and reaction terms.

4. Boundary Conditions

The first release supports the boundary-condition families used in the textbook transient examples and course notes: prescribed bottom and top values, a prescribed hot-face value with no flux through the back face, or fixed bottom with convective exchange at the top surface.

\[ c(0,t)=c_b(t), \qquad c(H,t)=c_t(t) \] \[ \frac{\partial c}{\partial y}\bigg|_{0}=0, \qquad c(H,t)=c_s \] \[ -D\frac{\partial c}{\partial y}\bigg|_{H} = h\left[c(H,t)-c_{\infty}(t)\right] \]

UI units on this page: c in mol/μm³; thickness and position in μm; time in hr; D in μm²/hr; volumetric source in mol/(μm³·hr); Robin transfer coefficient in μm/hr; swelling coefficient βswell in μm³/mol.

5. Time Integration and Output

The assembled equations are advanced with backward Euler for robustness with fine meshes and sharp transients. The solver steps exactly onto requested output times and returns the full position-value profile at each one.

\[ \left([C]+\Delta t[K]\right)\{c\}^{n+1} = [C]\{c\}^{n} + \Delta t\{s\}^{n+1} \]

These time-resolved profiles drive the optional stress coupling step: the swelling mismatch strain \(\theta(y,t) = \beta_{\mathrm{swell}}\,(c(y,t)-c_{\mathrm{ref}})\) is evaluated at every node and passed to the Milestone 1 multilayer stress solver to produce stress and curvature histories.

Verification Objective

For \(h_f \ll h_s\), the exact multilayer curvature should approach Stoney's approximation:

\[ \kappa_{\mathrm{Stoney}} = \frac{6\sigma_f h_f}{\bar{E}_s h_s^2}, \qquad \bar{E}_s = \frac{E_s}{1-\nu_s} \]
